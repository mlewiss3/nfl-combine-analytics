# NFL Combine Analytics

Analyzing which NFL combine metrics actually predict career success 
for cornerbacks, safeties, and wide receivers.

## Research Question
Do measurable athletic traits from the NFL Combine — 40-yard dash, 
vertical jump, broad jump, 3-cone drill, and short shuttle — 
statistically predict career performance for CB, S, and WR?

## Motivation
As a high school athlete who plays cornerback, safety, and wide receiver, 
I wanted to know whether the same physical metrics used to evaluate 
players at my positions actually translate to NFL success — or whether 
conventional wisdom overstates their importance.

## Methods
- Data: NFL combine results and career stats via nflreadpy
- Tools: Python, pandas, Jupyter Notebook, Streamlit
- Analysis: Correlation analysis, regression modeling
- Positions: Cornerback (CB), Safety (S), Wide Receiver (WR)

## Project Structure
- combine_analysis.ipynb — main analysis notebook
- combine_data_cb_s_wr.csv — filtered combine data (2,715 players)
- player_season_stats.csv — career stats (10,124 player seasons)
